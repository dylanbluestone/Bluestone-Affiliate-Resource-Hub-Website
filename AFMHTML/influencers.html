Mental Health Messaging Ethics

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Mental Health is Serious

When discussing mental health, we have a responsibility to be ethical and accurate. Always position Bluestone as a supportive tool, NOT a replacement for professional medical care.

Key Ethical Principles

  • Never claim Bluestone replaces therapy - It's a companion tool, not a substitute for professional care
  • Use person-first language - "person with depression" not "depressed person"
  • Avoid romanticizing mental illness - Don't make it seem aesthetic or trendy
  • Include crisis resources when needed - If discussing serious topics, provide hotline numbers
  • Be authentic about your journey - Share real experiences, not fabricated stories
  • Acknowledge individual differences - Results vary by person, don't over-promise
  • Encourage professional help - Always recommend therapy/doctors for serious concerns

FTC Disclosure Requirements

üö® LEGALLY REQUIRED

Affiliate disclosure is NOT optional - it's required by law (FTC regulations). Failure to disclose can result in fines and legal action. Always err on the side of over-disclosure.

You MUST clearly disclose your affiliate relationship with Bluestone in ALL promotional content. Here's how to stay compliant:

Required Disclosures by Platform

  • Instagram Posts: Include "#ad" or "#affiliate" in the FIRST LINE of your caption, before any line breaks
  • Instagram Stories: Use the "Paid Partnership" tag OR add text disclosure directly on screen
  • TikTok: Enable "Paid Partnership" toggle AND include "#ad" in caption or video text
  • YouTube: Check "Includes paid promotion" box AND verbally disclose in video AND mention in description
  • Twitter/X: Include "#ad" or "affiliate link" within the tweet itself, NOT just in profile

Disclosure Examples

Instagram: "#ad #affiliate - This post contains affiliate links. I earn a commission if you sign up."
